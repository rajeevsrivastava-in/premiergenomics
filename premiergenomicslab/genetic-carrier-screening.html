CYSTIC FIBROSIS
SCREENING

Cystic Fibrosis (CF): This is the most common life-threatening genetic disease in United States. People with CF inherit a defective gene that causes the body to produce abnormally thick mucus in the lungs, pancreas, and other organs.

Quick Facts:

30,000 people are affected by CF in the United States with 1 in 29 Caucasians being carriers of this gene.

Inheritance Factors

If both parents are carriers of an abnormal CF gene, then there is a 25% chance each parent will pass the gene on to their child. Having two copies of the abnormal CF gene will result in a 1 in 4 (25%) chance that the child will have CF.

Carrier & Frequency Rates

American College of Obstetrics and Gynecology recommends CF screening for all women planning to have a child or whom are currently pregnant.

Premier Genomics will assess your risk level with high accuracy and provide both you and your doctor with the information and knowledge necessary to better prepare you for the future.

FAQs

I have no family history of Cystic Fibrosis. Am I still at risk?

Yes, the reality is that any woman of childbearing age could be a carrier for Cystic Fibrosis, a common genetic condition. If undetected, carriers can pass this condition on to their unborn child. Premier Genomics will assess your actual risk with high accuracy and provide you and your doctor with the knowledge to better prepare for the future.

How is the test performed?

Cystic Fibrosis (CF) Screening is performed by a simple 5 minute buccal swab.

What if I am positive for CF gene?

If the test results show one partner is a carrier of CF, the next step is to test the other partner. Both parents must be carriers for the child to be at risk of inheriting Cystic Fibrosis.

When will I get the results?

Your doctor should receive the report analysis within 7-10 business days.

How much does it cost?

Most insurances will cover CF carrier screening.
