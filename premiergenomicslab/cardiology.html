If you are a patient afflicted with one of these illnesses, increase a dosage or switch to another medication due to side effects. With this genetic test, your doctor will be able to better understand how you metabolize certain medications. This helps to eliminate the old-fashioned “trial and error” approach to prescribing.

One of the main reasons for this unpredictability is the differences found in the genetic profiles that affect drug metabolism.

In fact, the United States Food and Drug Administration (FDA) recommends genetic testing for many types of drugs commonly prescribed for cardiovascular conditions including:

  • Carvediol
  • Clopidogrel
  • Metoprolol
  • Prasugrel
  • Propafenone
  • Propanolol
  • Ticagrelor










Drug optimization

Research shows that over 75% of drugs work in less than 50% of individuals. One of the main reasons for this is due to the different genetic make up of drug metabolism.

Our cardiology genetic panel to evaluate these drugs include:

  • CYP2C19
  • CYP2D6
  • CYP2C9
  • VKORC1
  • CYP3A4
  • CYP3A5
  • SLCO1B1










Other Cardiovascular Risk Assessments:

In addition to the cardiology panel above, Premier Genomics can assess other important risk factors including:

  • Thromophilia: Risk of thrombosis based upon mutations of Factor II or Factor V Leiden
  • Hyperhomocysteinemia: Risk of elevated homocsyteine based upon mutation of MTHFR
  • Hyperlipidemia/Atherosclerotic Vascular disease: Risk of elevated lipid and vascular disease based upon mutation of APOE
